Mercury Weather 3.0

Coming for iOS and macOS on Oct 2nd, 2025

This release introduces hurricane tracking, along with new daily weather details, including 24-hour forecasts for air quality, UV, and 48-hour tide charts.

  • Hurricane Tracking: The new storm layer in the map screen shows live hurricane information, including the current position, forecast path, and cone of uncertainty. The storm path is segmented into color-coded intensity scales (from Tropical Depression to Category 5), with selectable data points across the path showing detailed data like speed, pressure, and movement. The storm glyphs on the map also spin based on the intensity level of the storm and rotate based on hemisphere.
  • Hurricane Widgets: This update also added a new hurricane widget. Users can choose to either track a specific storm live or monitor the closest storm in a selected basin (Atlantic, Pacific, or both).
  • UV and Air Quality Forecasts: Mercury now includes a 24-hour UV forecast chart, accessible from the UV label in the top current weather card. We also show air quality forecasts whenever the AQI is expected to reach moderate or higher levels.
  • Location-Relevant Tide Changes (US & Canada): This update adds a 48-hour tide forecast that appears when viewing weather near coastal locations, where it‚Äôs most useful. Perfect for planning a day on the water or a walk along the beach.

Mercury Weather 2.10

Released on iOS and macOS on May 22nd, 2025

  • Radar: This update introduces our most requested feature, Radar. Alongside all the essentials of a radar map, the Mercury radar incorporates other features from the app to give it its own look and feel. The live radar shows real-time maps with radar and weather layers, perfect for people wanting extra insights by looking at radar and precipitation movement throughout the day. This is great if you're planning a day outside, or a road trip, and considering different routes.
  • Favorite Locations: The radar also shows you pins for your saved favorite locations, so you can select and view the current weather conditions and temperature at all your saved locations. Building on our Trip Forecasts feature, the map also shows all your upcoming trip destinations with travel dates.
  • New Widget: For those wanting a quick look at the current radar, our new Radar Widget has you covered, giving you access to the weather map, right on your home screen.

Mercury Weather 2.0

Release Date: August 30th, 2023

What's New?

Mercury 2.0 introduces Trip Forecast! This one's been in the making for a while, and we're so excited to finally release it.

Trip Forecast lets users enter their upcoming trips in Mercury. Once the trip is coming up, the weather at the destination will automatically show as part of the daily forecast timeline.

As part of this update, we've also added our first batch of custom app icons, added more weather details to the daily forecast, and improved a bunch of other things!

Some Background

Trip Forecast is a feature that started (like so many) with the question, "How hard could it be?". With a trip coming up, we thought it was kind of silly that our daily forecast showed the weather for where we were right then rather than where we'd be during our trip. The plan was to quickly build the feature in a week or two‚Ä¶ Fast forward a "brief" six months, and here we are, finally ready to ship it.

The feature evolved quite a lot as we used the prototype builds over the months. We're now supporting multi-day and multi-leg trips (even for trips that cross time zones and date boundaries), as well as day trips, including special behavior for arriving at a destination early, leaving late, or skipping a trip altogether.
